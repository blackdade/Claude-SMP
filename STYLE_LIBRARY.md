## VOICE RULES (non-negotiable)

**Sentence style:**
- Short. Punchy. One idea per sentence.
- Single-sentence paragraphs are fine — use them often.
- Write like talking to one smart person, not presenting to a room.
- Specific beats general always. "37 reactions in 2 hours" beats "strong early engagement."

**Tone:**
- Direct. Confident but not arrogant.
- Occasionally vulnerable (the 3am edits, the birthday post, the "am I doing this right" moments) — these humanise everything else.
- Never corporate. Never polished-sounding. Never guru-speak.
- Uses "we" when talking about Rillix Media as a team. Uses "I" for personal takes and founder journey.

**What Sanjay does NOT do:**
- No "In today's fast-paced world..."
- No "Excited to share..."
- No "Let's dive in!"
- No "Game-changing" or "Revolutionary"
- No em dashes — uses "—" only as a divider between sections, not mid-sentence
- No bullet walls — numbered lists with bold headers and a line break between each point
- No corporate hedging ("it could be argued that...")
- No generic CTAs like "What do you think?" — always specific

## POST FORMATS (ranked by what performs for Sanjay)

### Format 1 — The System Breakdown (HIGHEST PERFORMER)
**When:** Sanjay has a process, playbook, or framework to share.
**Structure:**
```
[Contrarian opening line or pattern interrupt]
[1-2 line setup — why this matters]

[Numbered steps with bold headers]
1. Title
Explanation (2-4 lines max)

2. Title
Explanation

[Formula line: X → Y → Z]

[Engagement CTA: comment trigger word for DM]
[Repost ask]
[P.S. — one final punch]
```
**Best examples:** "Most podcasters think more episodes = more growth" (38 likes, 14 comments), "I studied 20+ viral startup launches" (8 likes, 6 comments, 4 reposts)

---

### Format 2 — The Big Signal Post (OUTLIER POTENTIAL)
**When:** A major company/VC does something that validates Sanjay's worldview.
**Structure:**
```
[Company] just [did something].
[What everyone thinks it means.]

[But here's what it actually signals — Sanjay's read]

[3 lessons or implications — bullet or numbered]

[Connect to Rillix Media's work — soft CTA]
```
**Best example:** a16z New Media post (101K impressions). OpenAI/TBPN post (13K impressions).
**Note:** These posts MUST be tied to a real news event and Sanjay must have a distinct take — not just reporting the news.

---

### Format 3 — The Founder Journey Post (RELATIONSHIP BUILDER)
**When:** Sharing a personal moment, milestone, struggle, or realisation.
**Structure:**
```
[Honest, vulnerable opening line]
[The contrast — what it looks like vs what it feels like]

[The shift — what changed in how Sanjay thinks]

[Universal truth that applies to readers too]
[Soft close — no hard CTA, just resonance]
```
**Best examples:** "Nobody told me building an agency at 23 would feel like this" (13 likes, 2 comments), "I turned 23 today" (6 likes, 6 comments)

---

### Format 4 — The Client Story / Proof Post (TRUST BUILDER)
**When:** Showing real results from Rillix work without bragging.
**Structure:**
```
[Start with the client's situation, not Sanjay's achievement]
[What actually happened — specific numbers if possible]

[The insight behind the result — the thing most people miss]

[What this means for the reader — do they have the same problem?]
[CTA]
```
**Best example:** "We had a client record their first podcast episode last month... 1 conversation. 1 hour. 15 assets."

---

### Format 5 — The Contrarian Take (ENGAGEMENT DRIVER)
**When:** Sanjay has a view that goes against the common advice in his niche.
**Structure:**
```
[Statement that challenges conventional wisdom — first line]
[1-line setup]

[The real situation — short paragraphs, build tension]

[The actual truth — Sanjay's position]

[Question to readers that forces a pick-a-side response]
```

## HOOK PATTERNS (what works for Sanjay)

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Contrast hook | "Most podcasters record 50 episodes a year. And stay invisible." |
| Pattern interrupt number | "95% of podcasters quit after 25 episodes." |
| Big claim + proof teaser | "I studied 20+ viral startup launches on LinkedIn. Some generated 3M+ views." |
| Reframe the problem | "VC funds think they have a deal flow problem when they actually have a visibility problem." |
| News peg + personal lens | "OpenAI didn't just buy a tech talk show. They bought the ultimate distribution moat." |
| Vulnerable opener | "Nobody told me building an agency at 23 would feel like this." |
| Counterintuitive stat | "A podcast is the only content format where someone spends 45-90 minutes with you." |

**Rule: The hook must work as a standalone sentence in the feed preview. Test it by reading just the first line. If it makes you want to tap "see more" — keep it. If not — rewrite.**

## CTA PATTERNS (what Sanjay uses)

**Comment trigger CTAs (highest conversion):**
- Comment "[WORD]" below and I'll [specific thing]
- Drop a comment with "[WORD]" and I'll send you [specific deliverable]

**Repost asks (use sparingly, only when genuinely useful to others):**
- ♻️ Repost if you know a [specific person] who needs this.

**Question CTAs (for engagement, not lead gen):**
- [Specific question that requires a pick-a-side answer, not a general "what do you think"]

**Never use:** "Like and follow for more." "Share your thoughts below." "What do you think?"
