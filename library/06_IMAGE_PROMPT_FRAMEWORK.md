# Scroll-Stopping Image Prompt Framework
## For Rillix Content Agent — Creative Asset Briefs
# Used by: every writer agent (LinkedIn, X, Substack) — read this before writing any IMAGE PROMPT
# Added 2026-07-04, supplied by Sanjay

> **Rule #1:** Never generate a flat quote-card or generic infographic. Every image must make someone stop scrolling.

---

## The Problem With Quote Cards

Plain text on a solid background is invisible in 2025+ feeds. Algorithms reward visual novelty. The styles below are what's currently stopping thumbs on X, LinkedIn, and Substack.

---

## STYLE LIBRARY — Pick Per Post

### Style 1: Cyberpunk Anime Workstation

**When to use:** Posts about building, grinding, late-night work, founder journey, shipping product, coding sessions.

**Visual DNA:** Overhead or 3/4 angle of a figure at a multi-monitor setup. Moody ambient lighting (blue/purple/green screen glow). Anime art style. Cluttered desk with energy drinks, cables, sticky notes. Rain on windows. CRT scan lines optional.

**Example prompt:**
```
Anime-style overhead shot of a young founder working alone at a cluttered desk with 3 glowing monitors showing code and dashboards, ambient blue and purple lighting, rain hitting the window behind them, energy drink cans scattered, cables everywhere, cyberpunk aesthetic, dark moody atmosphere, Studio Ghibli meets Akira style, aspect ratio 16:9, highly detailed
```

**Variants:**
- Swap monitors for podcast editing software / waveforms for Rillix-specific content
- Add a clock showing 2:47 AM for "grind" posts
- Switch to warm golden desk lamp lighting for "calm focus" tone

---

### Style 2: Data-Mosaic / Matrix Art

**When to use:** Posts about AI, content systems, automation, data, analytics, growth metrics.

**Visual DNA:** A recognizable object or figure composed of flowing data characters, code snippets, or pixel-mosaic patterns. Dark background. Object emerges from or dissolves into data streams. Think: the substance of the thing IS information.

**Example prompt:**
```
A lotus flower made entirely of flowing matrix-style data characters and code, cascading green and gold digital text forming petals, dark black background, a silhouette figure meditating above it, cinematic lighting, data visualization art style, high contrast, aspect ratio 16:9
```

**Variants:**
- Microphone made of data streams (for podcast/content posts)
- Brain or neural network formed from scrolling code
- City skyline built from dashboard metrics and chart fragments

---

### Style 3: Clean Futuristic Product Lineup

**When to use:** Comparison posts, "landscape of X" posts, tool reviews, industry roundups, listicles.

**Visual DNA:** Objects or figures lined up on a clean white/light grey surface. Soft studio lighting. Each item distinct but cohesive. Brand logos below optional. Think Apple product photography meets robotics expo.

**Example prompt:**
```
Clean studio photograph of 5 different podcast microphones arranged in a lineup on a white surface, each a different style and color, soft diffused lighting, minimalist product photography, brand-neutral, slight reflection on surface, aspect ratio 16:9, photorealistic
```

**Variants:**
- Abstract icons representing different tools/platforms instead of real products
- Isometric 3D renders of app interfaces lined up
- Silhouettes of different "types" of founders/creators

---

### Style 4: Cinematic Split-Screen / Diptych

**When to use:** Before/after posts, comparison takes, "X vs Y" frameworks, transformation stories.

**Visual DNA:** Screen split vertically or diagonally. Left side = chaos/old way (desaturated, messy, dim). Right side = new way (vibrant, clean, lit). Strong visual contrast tells the story without text.

**Example prompt:**
```
Cinematic split-screen image: left half shows a chaotic messy desk with scattered papers and sticky notes in desaturated grey tones, right half shows a clean minimal workspace with a single glowing screen showing organized dashboards in vibrant blue and teal, dramatic diagonal split line with light bleeding through, aspect ratio 16:9, photorealistic cinematic lighting
```

---

### Style 5: Neon Typography on Texture

**When to use:** Bold opinion posts, hot takes, single-line punchy statements. The ONLY acceptable "text on image" style.

**Visual DNA:** NOT a flat quote card. Text rendered as neon signage, embossed metal, etched glass, chalk on blackboard, spray paint on concrete, or LED display. The text has physical presence in a real or rendered environment.

**Example prompt:**
```
A neon sign reading "CONTENT IS THE NEW DEAL FLOW" mounted on a dark exposed brick wall, glowing electric blue with subtle pink reflection, moody bar atmosphere, slight fog/haze, shot from slight angle not straight-on, photorealistic, aspect ratio 16:9
```

**Variants:**
- LED ticker board in a stock exchange setting
- Carved into stone/marble for "timeless truth" posts
- Handwritten chalk on a blackboard for "lesson learned" posts
- Spray-painted on a concrete wall for "rebellious take" posts

---

### Style 6: Isometric World / Mini-Scene

**When to use:** System explanations, workflow posts, "how it works" breakdowns, process posts.

**Visual DNA:** Isometric 3D illustration of a tiny world or machine. Clean vector or soft 3D render style. Shows a process or system as a physical scene. Think: Notion/Linear illustration style but more detailed.

**Example prompt:**
```
Isometric 3D illustration of a content production factory: raw podcast audio enters on a conveyor belt on the left, passes through machines labeled with icons for editing, clipping, and distribution, finished short-form videos exit on the right onto different platform conveyor belts, soft pastel colors with electric blue accents, clean minimal style, white background, aspect ratio 1:1
```

---

### Style 7: Dramatic Silhouette / Solitary Figure

**When to use:** Founder journey posts, reflection pieces, "lonely at the top" moments, personal story posts.

**Visual DNA:** Single figure silhouetted against a dramatic backdrop — city skyline, sunrise, vast landscape, glowing screen. Emphasizes scale, solitude, determination. Cinematic color grading.

**Example prompt:**
```
Silhouette of a young man standing on a rooftop looking out at a sprawling city skyline at golden hour, warm orange and deep purple sky, city lights beginning to glow below, cinematic wide shot, dramatic backlighting, the figure is small against the vast cityscape, aspect ratio 16:9, film photography aesthetic
```

## PLATFORM-SPECIFIC SIZING

| Platform | Aspect Ratio | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| **X (Twitter)** | 16:9 | Dominates feed. Avoid 1:1 — it looks small on X. |
| **LinkedIn** | 1:1 or 4:5 | 4:5 takes up more feed real estate on mobile. |
| **Substack** | 16:9 or 3:2 | Header images should be 16:9. In-body can be wider. |

**This supersedes any earlier aspect-ratio guidance in this repo** — LinkedIn was previously specified as 16:9 and X as 1:1 in STYLE_LIBRARY.md and the writer agents; those are wrong and corrected by this table as of 2026-07-04.

## NEVER DO

- Flat solid-color background with centered text (the old quote-card)
- Stock photo of a handshake, laptop, or coffee cup
- Generic "AI brain" or "network nodes" illustrations
- Anything that looks like a Canva template
- Text-heavy images with more than 8 words
- Corporate blue gradient backgrounds
- Overused Midjourney "epic cinematic" with no specific direction

## ALWAYS DO

- Match style to post theme (see style-to-post mapping below)
- Include specific environmental details in prompts (weather, time of day, objects)
- Specify art style explicitly (anime, photorealistic, isometric, etc.)
- Specify lighting direction and mood
- Use the platform-correct aspect ratio
- Add one unexpected detail that creates visual intrigue
- When using text in images, give it physical material presence (neon, carved, projected)

**Variant A in the IMAGE PROMPT block = the Primary Style prompt for that post's theme. Variant B = the Backup Style prompt.** These should be genuinely different visual approaches, not two small tweaks of the same concept.

## PROMPT STRUCTURE TEMPLATE

Use this structure for every image prompt:
```
[Art style] + [Subject/scene description] + [Specific environmental details] +
[Lighting/mood] + [Color palette keywords] + [One unexpected detail] +
[aspect ratio for platform] + [quality keywords]
```

**Quality keywords to always append:** `highly detailed, trending on artstation, sharp focus, professional`

For Midjourney add: `--ar 16:9 --v 6` (or appropriate ratio)
For Ideogram add: aspect ratio in the prompt text itself
