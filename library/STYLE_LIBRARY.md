## VOICE RULES (apply to ALL platforms, non-negotiable)

- Short, punchy sentences. 1-3 lines per paragraph max.
- Single-sentence paragraphs are fine — often better.
- Write like talking to one smart person, not presenting to a room.
- Specific always beats general. Numbers, names, results — never vague claims.
- Never corporate. Never polished-sounding. Never guru-speak.
- Uses "we" when speaking as Rillix Media. Uses "I" for personal takes.
- No em dashes mid-sentence — only as a section divider.
- No rhetorical questions as hooks.
- No generic CTAs ("What do you think?" / "Drop your thoughts below").

**Banned phrases (any platform):**
"In today's landscape / fast-paced world" · "Let's dive in" · "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary" / "Groundbreaking" · "I'm excited to share" · "Thrilled to announce" · "At the end of the day" · "The bottom line is" · "It's no secret that" · Sycophantic openers of any kind.

**Persuasion structure:** Apply 05_PERSUASION_WRITING_SKILL.md (Dan Koe method) to argument structure and CTA writing on every platform. When the persuasion framework and Sanjay's voice pull in different directions, **Sanjay's voice always wins.** The framework governs structure, not tone.

## PLATFORM RULES

### LinkedIn
- Post 1-2x per day.
- Split: 60-70% podcast/media authority content, 30-40% product launch and startup content.
- Formats (ranked by performance below): System Breakdown, Big Signal Post, Founder Journey, Client Story, Contrarian Take.
- Hashtags: 4-8 at the end. Always include at least two of #podcast #VC #founder #Startups.
- Best performing format: numbered system breakdown + comment-trigger CTA.

### X (Twitter)
- Post 1-2x per day.
- Split: 50-60% product launch and startup content, 30-40% podcast content.
- Format is auto-selected by topic, not chosen manually:
  - Big launch or news → short punchy single post, 1-3 lines
  - System or insight with multiple points → thread (1/ 2/ 3/ format)
  - Hot take or reaction → 1-3 lines, no thread
  - Video post → short text + video callout
- Shorter and punchier than LinkedIn — X rewards brevity, not completeness.
- Hashtags: 0-2 max, or none. Never a hashtag wall.
- Threads: every tweet must stand alone AND connect to the next.

### Substack
- Post 2-3x per week — only when a topic genuinely warrants it, never to fill a quota.
- Split: 40% personal/founder journey, 30% industry thought leadership (longer essays), 20% podcast expertise breakdowns, 10% motivational quote + Sanjay's real take.
- Format is decided by topic:
  - Personal moment → short note, 2-4 paragraphs, conversational, no headers
  - Industry signal + opinion → short article, 400-600 words, 1-2 headers max
  - Deep breakdown → full article, 800-1200 words, headers, structured argument
- More personal voice than LinkedIn or X.
- Apply the Dan Koe persuasion framework more fully here — longer form allows deeper argument structure.
- No hard sales CTA. Soft close only: "If this is the problem you have, you know where to find me."

## LINKEDIN POST FORMATS (ranked by performance)

### 1. System Breakdown (HIGHEST PERFORMER)
**When:** Sanjay has a process, playbook, or framework to share.
```
[Contrarian opening line or pattern interrupt]
[1-2 line setup — why this matters]

[Numbered steps with bold headers]
1. Title
Explanation (2-4 lines max)

2. Title
Explanation

[Formula line: X → Y → Z]

[Comment-trigger CTA]
[Repost ask]
[P.S. — one final punch]
```
**Examples:** "Most podcasters think more episodes = more growth" (38 likes, 14 comments), "I studied 20+ viral startup launches" (early traction, strong reposts).

### 2. Big Signal Post (OUTLIER POTENTIAL)
**When:** A major company/VC does something that validates Sanjay's worldview.
```
[Company] just [did something].
[What everyone thinks it means.]

[But here's what it actually signals — Sanjay's read]

[3 lessons or implications]

[Connect to Rillix's work — soft CTA]
```
**Examples:** a16z New Media post (101,984 impressions — biggest outlier to date), OpenAI/TBPN post (13,043 impressions). Must be tied to a real news event with a distinct take — never just reporting.

### 3. Founder Journey (RELATIONSHIP BUILDER)
**When:** Personal moment, milestone, struggle, or realisation.
```
[Honest, vulnerable opening line]
[The contrast — what it looks like vs what it feels like]

[The shift — what changed in how Sanjay thinks]

[Universal truth that applies to readers too]
[Soft close — no hard CTA]
```

### 4. Client Story / Proof Post (TRUST BUILDER)
**When:** Showing real results without bragging.
```
[Start with the client's situation, not Sanjay's achievement]
[What actually happened — specific numbers]

[The insight behind the result]

[What this means for the reader]
[CTA]
```
**Example:** Client recorded one podcast episode. One conversation, one hour. Fifteen assets.

### 5. Contrarian Take (ENGAGEMENT DRIVER)
**When:** A view that goes against common advice in the niche.
```
[Statement that challenges conventional wisdom]
[1-line setup]

[The real situation — short paragraphs, build tension]

[The actual truth — Sanjay's position]

[Pick-a-side question]
```

## HOOK PATTERNS

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Contrast hook | "Most podcasters record 50 episodes a year. And stay invisible." |
| Pattern interrupt number | "95% of podcasters quit after 25 episodes." |
| Big claim + proof teaser | "I studied 20+ viral startup launches. Some generated 3M+ views." |
| Reframe the problem | "VC funds think they have a deal flow problem when they actually have a visibility problem." |
| News peg + personal lens | "OpenAI didn't just buy a tech talk show. They bought the ultimate distribution moat." |
| Vulnerable opener | "Nobody told me building an agency at 23 would feel like this." |
| Counterintuitive stat | "A podcast is the only content format where someone spends 45-90 minutes with you." |

Rule: the hook must work standalone in the feed preview. Read only the first line — if it doesn't earn "see more," rewrite it.

**Rotate patterns — never reuse the same one two posts in a row, on any platform.** Before writing a hook, check what pattern the previous post (this platform, and ideally the last few days) used and pick a different one from this table. A negation opener ("No, it's not X") is one option here, not the default — see 05_PERSUASION_WRITING_SKILL.md's Opening Move section for when it's the right call versus when a different pattern will land harder. The goal is that each post reads like its own piece of writing, not a template with the topic swapped in.

## CTA PATTERNS

**Comment trigger (highest conversion):**
- Comment "[WORD]" below and I'll [specific thing].

**Repost ask (sparingly, only when genuinely useful to others):**
- ♻️ Repost if you know a [specific person] who needs this.

**Pick-a-side question (engagement, not lead gen):**
- A specific question that forces a position — never "what do you think?"

**Never use:** "Like and follow for more." "Share your thoughts below." "What do you think?"

## CREATIVE ASSET BRIEF (every single draft ends with this)

```
CREATIVE ASSET:
Type: [Talking head / Graphic / Voiceover / None]
Required: [Yes — post needs this / Optional / No]
Duration: [e.g., 45-60 seconds] (if video)
Visual direction: [B-roll moments, captions, cuts every 2-3 seconds, energy pacing]
Caption style: [Auto-captions only / Add B-roll at these moments: X, Y, Z]
```

**If Type is Talking head or Voiceover:** do not write the spoken script inline. Add this instead:

```
VIDEO SCRIPT TRIGGER:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Copy and paste this into Claude chat to get the full video script:

"Write a video script for this post: [post title/topic]
Platform: [LinkedIn/X/Substack]
Duration: [45-60 seconds / 60-90 seconds]
Format: [Talking head / Voiceover / Graphic video]
Follow the same structure and voice rules as my [platform] posts —
short punchy sentences, hook in first 3 seconds,
visual direction every line, strong CTA at end."
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

**If Type is Graphic, or any post uses a still image:** add this instead. Never a flat quote-card or generic infographic — pick a style from `06_IMAGE_PROMPT_FRAMEWORK.md`'s Style-to-Post Theme table, and follow its Prompt Structure Template and NEVER DO / ALWAYS DO rules:

```
IMAGE PROMPT (paste directly into Midjourney or Ideogram):
Style: [Primary style name from 06_IMAGE_PROMPT_FRAMEWORK.md]
[Full generation prompt — art style, subject/scene, environmental details,
lighting/mood, color palette, one unexpected detail, aspect ratio, quality keywords]

Variant A (Primary style): [full prompt in the theme's primary style]
Variant B (Backup style): [full prompt in the theme's backup style — a genuinely different visual approach]
```

Image prompts must be specific enough to generate on the first try. Aspect ratio by platform: **LinkedIn 1:1 or 4:5, X 16:9, Substack 16:9 or 3:2 (default 16:9).** See `06_IMAGE_PROMPT_FRAMEWORK.md` for the full style library, platform sizing table, and quality-keyword conventions.

Sanjay records talking head videos himself. Team edits with B-roll, captions, pacing. Graphic option for stat callouts and frameworks. Voiceover option for screen recordings or animated graphics.

## ENGAGEMENT SIGNALS FROM PAST POSTS

**What spiked:**
- a16z New Media post: news peg + strong original take + clear CTA = 101,984 impressions. The comment trigger "Media" drove 227 comments.
- Podcast breakdown post: numbered system + honest P.S. = 38 likes, 14 comments, 6 reposts — top System Breakdown performer.
- Launch playbook post: numbered system + specific proof = strong early reposts.

**What underperformed:**
- Reposts of others' content with short commentary — low reach consistently.
- Short motivational/quote-style posts with no system or proof — low engagement.
- Posts without a CTA or engagement hook — comments drop off fast.

**Key insight:** Sanjay's outlier posts all share three things — a news peg or contrarian claim, a numbered breakdown, and a comment-trigger CTA. That's the template to prioritise across LinkedIn and, in longer form, Substack.
