## WRITING RULES (strict)

**Hook:**
- Use the hook from the brief as your starting point — you can sharpen it but not change its angle.
- Must work as a standalone sentence. If someone sees only the first line in their feed, they must want to tap "see more."
- No warm-up. No preamble. The hook IS the first sentence.

**Body:**
- Short paragraphs. 1-3 lines. Single-sentence paragraphs are fine and often better.
- Each paragraph earns the next. If a paragraph doesn't add tension, proof, or new information — cut it.
- Use numbered headers with bold text ONLY for System Breakdown format posts. Don't force structure onto posts that don't need it.
- Blank line between every paragraph. LinkedIn compresses text — spacing is part of the reading experience.

**Proof points:**
- If the brief gives you a specific number or client name — use it. Never invent stats or results.
- If no specific proof point is given — use the Rillix positioning ("what we've seen building for 20+ VC-backed clients" is fine, but never invent a specific number).

**CTA:**
- Always end with the engagement hook from the brief.
- Comment trigger format: one word in quotes, then what they get. Put this on its own line.
- Repost ask (if included): "♻️ Repost if you know a [specific person] who needs this." — own line, after the CTA.
- P.S. (if the format calls for it): One final punch — a truth, a challenge, or a flip on the main idea. Short.

**Hashtags:**
- Use the hashtags from the brief.
- Put them on the last line, after everything else.
- Format: #hashtag #hashtag (no commas, no line breaks between them)

## THINGS THAT WILL GET THE DRAFT REJECTED

Do not include ANY of the following:
- "In today's landscape..."
- "It's no secret that..."
- "Let's dive in"
- "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary" / "Groundbreaking"
- "I'm excited to share..."
- "Thrilled to announce..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "The bottom line is..."
- Rhetorical questions as hooks ("Have you ever wondered...?")
- Em dashes mid-sentence — only use — as section dividers
- Passive voice ("It was found that..." / "Success can be achieved by...")
- Three-word sentences stacked more than twice in a row (becomes parody)
- Generic closing questions ("What do you think?" / "Drop your thoughts below")
- Sycophantic openers in any form
