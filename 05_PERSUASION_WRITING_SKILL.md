## CORE PRINCIPLE

Persuasion is structured thinking, not tone. A post persuades when it (1) states a position immediately, (2) removes the reader's easiest wrong interpretation before they can reach for it, and (3) makes the reader feel implicated, not informed.

Explain the idea like it's for a 5-year-old. Never write at a 5th-grade level. Simplify the idea, not the language.

---

## THE OPENING PATTERN (use on every post)

1. **State the conclusion first.** No setup, no context sentence before it. This is the Pyramid Principle: answer → argument → proof, never the reverse.
2. **Kill 1-2 likely misreadings immediately** with flat, short negations: "No, it's not X." "No, it's not Y." Do this *before* explaining what it actually is. This is what creates pull — the reader is told what a thing isn't before they're told what it is, which is a wider gap than just stating a fact.
3. **Deliver the reframe** — the real claim, stated flat.
4. **Add one more tension line before backing it up.** Do not explain immediately. Let the reframe sit for one beat.

Template:
```
[Bold, checkable claim.]

No, it's not [obvious wrong read].

No, it's not [second obvious wrong read].

[The real reframe, stated flat.]

[One line that raises stakes or implicates the reader — do not resolve yet.]
```

---

## DIRECT-ADDRESS RULE

Second person ("you") beats third person every time. "VCs have a visibility problem" is weaker than "You have a visibility problem." Point the claim at the reader, not at a category they can mentally exclude themselves from.

---

## FRAMEWORK ARSENAL (pick based on format)

**Pyramid Principle** (use for every post as the outer structure):
Conclusion → 3+ supporting arguments → each argument backed by a fact, number, or proof point. Never invent the proof — pull from STYLE_LIBRARY.md proof points.

**P-P (Pain → Process)** — short posts, single tweet-length ideas:
State the pain point. Give the step-by-step fix. Close with a line that reframes the pain as caused by a system, not the reader's failure.

**PASO (Problem → Amplify → Solution → Offer)** — when there's a CTA/offer to make:
Problem (1-2 lines) → Amplify what it costs them specifically → Solution (the fix) → Offer (soft link/CTA).

**PASTOR (+ Story, Testimony)** — long-form only (newsletter, landing page, not LinkedIn):
Problem → Amplify → Story → Testimony/proof → Offer → Response ask.

**5 Levels of Awareness** — calibrates how much you need to explain:
1. Unaware (pain point only) → 2. Problem aware (amplify impact) → 3. Solution aware (give the fix) → 4. Product aware (your unique method) → 5. Most aware (just needs the right angle to act).
LinkedIn posts should sit 80% in levels 1-3. Don't write like the reader is already sold.

---

## TEACHING-BY-EXPOSURE (optional, use sparingly)

When useful, name the mechanism while using it ("notice this is the reframe, not the resolution"). Builds trust because the reader watches the technique work on them in real time. Use only in System Breakdown posts — never in Founder Journey or Big Signal posts, where it breaks the tone.
